Egyptian Koshary Dish

Welcome to this egyptian recipe page! Koshary is Egypt's national dish and a widely popular street food. It is a traditional Egyptian staple, mixing pasta, Egyptian fried rice, vermicelli and brown lentils, and topped with chickpeas, a garlicky tomato sauce, garlic vinegar, and crispy fried onions. Sprinklings of garlic vinegar and hot sauce are optional.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • lentils
  • macaroni
  • Vermicelli
  • tomato sauce
  • vegetable oil
  • onions
  • cumin
  • coriander

Instructions

  1. Fry the onions in oil until golden brown, then remove and drain them on paper towels.
  2. Boil the lentils in water with some salt and cumin, preferably soaking them for at least three hours.
  3. Fry the vermicelli until it turns a light brown color and is evenly distributed, then mix the rice with the black lentils.
  4. Add this mixture to the vermicelli, then pour in boiling water with some salt and cumin, and cover the pot.
  5. Once most of the water is absorbed, reduce the heat to prevent burning.
  6. Boil the pasta in boiling water with some salt and one or two tablespoons of oil.
  7. Combine the pasta with the rice, lentils, and vermicelli, and serve with tomato sauce, fried onions, chickpeas, and dukkah.
