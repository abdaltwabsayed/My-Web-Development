The Three Holy Mosques in Islam

The "Holy Mosques" in Islam refer to the three most sacred sites for Muslims: Al-Masjid al-Haram in Mecca (housing the Kaaba), Al-Masjid an-Nabawi (The Prophet's Mosque) in Medina, and Al-Masjid al-Aqsa in Jerusalem, a pilgrimage to these three being highly significant. These sites are central to Islamic faith, pilgrimage (Hajj/Umrah), and history, with Al-Masjid al-Haram being the primary focus for prayer direction (Qibla).

Masjid al-Haram

Masjid al-Haram, also known as the Sacred Mosque or the Great Mosque of Mecca, is considered to be the most significant mosque in Islam. It encloses the vicinity of the Kaaba in Mecca, in the western region of Saudi Arabia. It is among the pilgrimage sites associated with the Hajj, which every Muslim must perform at least once in their lives if able. It is also the main site for the performance of ʿUmrah, the lesser pilgrimage that can be undertaken at any time of the year. The rites of both pilgrimages include circumambulating the Kaaba within the mosque. The Great Mosque contains other important sites, such as the Black Stone, the Zamzam Well, Maqam Ibrahim, and the hills of Safa and Marwa.

Prophet's Mosque

The Prophet's Mosque is the second mosque built by the Islamic prophet Muhammad in Medina, after the Quba Mosque, and is also the second-largest mosque and holiest site in Islam, after the Masjid al-Haram in Mecca, in the Saudi region of the Hejaz. The mosque is located at the heart of Medina, and is a major site of pilgrimage that falls under the purview of the Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques (a royal style used by the King of Saudi Arabia).

Al-Aqsa Mosque

Al-Aqsa Mosque is a mosque in the Old City of Jerusalem. It is the third holiest site in Islam. Muslims believe that the mosque was originally built by the Islamic prophet Muhammad, who led a prayer congregation there during his Night Journey (Isra and Mi'raj). It is also believed to be the place where Muhammad ascended to heaven (Mi'raj). The mosque is located on the Temple Mount, known to Muslims as Haram al-Sharif (the Noble Sanctuary), which also includes the Dome of the Rock.
